Brief

We started with a concept, before a team was built, to create clips of video from Twitch.tv live streams. From here we refined the product to its present by drawing directly from the fragments of binary video data. Then developed a high scale, high performance architecture to deliver this content to users. This project lead to industry-wide developments to support clips from live streams.

Process

We started with a rough concept, with only a few libraries and pre-existing products to consider. We decided instead to build a system from scratch, since no other similar product existed. This lead to a substantially higher quality product than was originally planned. We then built on top of Amazon Web Services (EC2, S3, CloudFront, Elastic Transcoder) to deliver a high scale delivery and high performance, rapid video compilation.

Result

Oddshot was instantly popular for delivering, with extraordinary speed, a compiled and transcoded video clip for users to enjoy. We compiled and transcoded several thousands of clips per day and delivered video content to millions of users per month.
